Head Structure
- First, get really good at drawing circles. I know, it's boring, but it will pay off.
- Next, draw a curved line to complete the sphere, and a straight line extending down. Finally, draw the jawline.
- Also draw the lines for the eyes, nose, and mouth following the half-half-half rule (the eyes are half of the head or at the top of the ears, the nose is halfway between the eyes and chin, and the mouth is halfway between the nose and chin.
- Remember that the head is roughly a sphere, so draw curved lines around the head for the features, especially with the 3/4 view.

Hair
When drawing hair, it's best to draw or imaging the origin of the hair, and draw all strands eminating from or pointing to that origin.
Practice curving lines like a strand of hair --> Think aloe vera leaf crossed with a banana, curved and pointed.
Draw hair strand not as each individual, but as a group of strands. Vary the size and width.
Obviously, you should erase the origin point, hairline guidelines, etc.
